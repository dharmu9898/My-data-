What is Alopecia?

Alopecia is a medical terminology for different types of hair loss.


What is alopecia aerated?

As per national alopecia aerate foundation, alopecia aerate (al-oh-PEE-shah air-eel-AH-tah) is a highly unpredictable, autoimmune skin disease resulting in the loss of hair on the scalp and elsewhere on the body. This common but very challenging and capricious disease affects approximately 1.7 percent of the population. Due to the fact that much of the public is still not familiar with alopecia aerate, the disease can have a profound impact on one's life and functional status, both at work and at school.
In alopecia aerate, the affected hair follicles are mistakenly attacked by a person's own immune system (white blood cells), resulting in the arrest of the hair growth stage. Alopecia aerate usually starts with one or more small, round, smooth bald patched on the scalp and can progress to total scalp hair loss (alopecia totals) or complete body hair loss (alopecia universalism).


What is alopecia as per ayurveda?

AYURVEDA has described hair disease in three words which are as under


  • KHALITYA: means loss of hairs
  • PALITYA : means prematured hair graying
  • INDRAPULTA: means alopecia aerate, totals, universalism.
