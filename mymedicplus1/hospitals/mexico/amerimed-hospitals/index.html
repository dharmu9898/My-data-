Amerimed Hospitals

AmeriMed is a network of medical facilities in Mexico that provide full medical services based upon U.S. standards of health care.

They offer a full range of emergency and routine services round the clock with a well staffed bilingual support. In-house specialists and physicians in emergency medicine are available. Their nurses provide total quality care, supported by the latest in technology.

AmeriMed is a recognized leader and preferred professional provider of optimal medical service and care for residents and travelers, both domestic and International.

The first AmeriMed Hospital opened in Puerto Vallarta in the fall of 1996. This was followed by the opening of hospitals in Cabo San Lucas, Los Cabos, in March 2001, a Clinic in Los Barriles (Baja California Sur) in March 2006, and soon a New Hospital in San José del Cabo (2007).

AmeriMed offers an integrated medical system which includes urgent and emergency care, obstetrics and gynecology care, family practice, and medical and surgical diagnosis and treatment.

The services are delivered with comfort, security, and personal touch.

They have proven expertise in emergency medicine, and are proud of the delivery of the same high quality care for routine medical and surgical problems as well.

The Facilities are staffed 24 hours a day, 7 days a week with bilingual, in-house physicians.

Many of our patients opt to have reconstructive or aesthetic surgery at our spa-like setting. This very discreet and private service is exclusively available at AmeriMed.


Cardiac Catherization Lab at AmeriMed American Hospital - Cabo San Lucas, Mexico.

Los Cabos residents have the advantage of having access to cardiac and endovascular studies and treatments locally through the services of Amerimed Hospitals. This department is directed by the well renowned bilingual invasive cardiologist Dr. Rafael Moguel, who trained in Barcelona, Spain. Dr. Moguel and his team perform diagnostic angiography, coronary angioplasty, and coronary artery stenting. He and his team also diagnosis and treat some of the most common cardiovalvular and congenital diseases of the heart. In the area of peripheral vascular diseases, he performs carotid artery stenting and peripheral vascular atherectomies, which in some cases, may prevent limb amputation.
He and his team perform an average of two hundred of these various procedures per year, having done so for more than thirteen years. Dr. Moguel continues to keep abreast of advancements in his medical field, attending major conferences in some of the largest medical centers in the USA and Europe.
Dr. Moguel is a recognized member of the most important Latin American and International Societies of Interventional Cardiology and Endovascular Specialists. He is a fellow of the Society of Cardiac Angiography and Intervention. He is accredited by the Mexican Council of Cardiology to perform endovascular diagnosis and treatment.

Cardiac Catheterization in Cabo
Patient: Robert Moore
Diagnoses: Unstable angina
Treatment: Angioplasty plus stenting
May 18, 2006

To whom it may concern:
Words cannot adequately describe how traumatic it is to be in a foreign country and experience having severe chest pain where you know already that you have two stents and are taking 8 medications daily to ward off further heart problems. It was a blessing to have the taxi pull up to AmeriMed and to see the words “American Hospital”. From the moment we rolled in through the doors until now as I prepare to leave, my family and I were treated with extremely competent care and compassion. I was seen immediately and also very quickly given medicine that alleviate my pain and calm me. The doctors took the time to learn mi cardiac history and called my doctor in the USA to confer whit them. I really appreciate them taking the extra time to do this. While my initial choice would have been to have my own cardiologist care for me, the cost of air ambulance was prohibitive. However, I am glad that we chose to stay and am very grateful that Dr. Montes Marin was able locate the doctor who come to do my procedure.
All of the medical staff far exceeded my expectations. I can truly say that I feel my care here was equal to or better than the care I received in the US during my stay in the hospital there. We experienced very little problems with language barriers as most of the staff are competent in English even though we don’t speak or understand Spanish.
The only area of concern has been the payment of account. Because I am covered under my employers’ insurance which is an excellent policy (Duke University Medical Center) we never have to prepay any charges. This was new for us and caused some stress until we were able to get word from the insurance company that this was procedure here and that they would assist us with paperwork when we got back home. I suggest that the business office assist patients make those connections with insurance companies earlier in the process or that the hospital works towards being able to direct bill insurance electronically so that patients only pay a portion while in Mexico.
Overall, were had and excellent experience, and I am 100% satisfied with the care and service I received at AmeriMed.

For your compassion
