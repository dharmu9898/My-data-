Albert Einstein Sociedade Beneficente Israelita Brasileira

Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein relies on qualified human resources, state-of-the-art equipment and, more importantly, attention, respect and agility to offer quality health care. We are prepared to tailor our services to the needs of each and every patient to guarantee excellence and a positive experience.

With respect and professionalism, HIAE achieved a leading position in Latin America. It has been accredited for three consecutive times by the Joint Commission International and possesses a number of ISO certifications. The principle of continuous improvement and excellence is shared among all those who contribute to our work, from medical staff to scientists and management.

History

In 1955, a group of idealists from the Jewish community of São Paulo founded Sociedade Beneficente Israelita Brasileira Albert Einstein (SBIBAE), based upon four traditional Jewish principles:

  • Good Deeds (Mitzvá)
  • Health, Healing (Refuá)
  • Education (Chinuch)
  • Solidarity, Justice (Tsedaká)

Over half a century later, SBIBAE has become a great and modern institution, governed by high quality standards and in tune with the most advanced technologies available.


Highlights

Mission

To offer quality and excellence in health care through medical assistance, knowledge generation and social responsibility, as a means to demonstrate the contribution of the Jewish Community to the Brazilian people.

Vision

To be leaders and innovators in medical and hospital care, a benchmark in knowledge management, and recognized for our commitment to social responsibility.

Values

Mitzvot (Good Deeds), Refuah (Health), Chinuch (Education) and Tzedakah (Social Justice) are the Jewish values that motivated doctors from the Jewish community to found the Society more than 50 years ago. Alongside the organizational values of Honesty, Truthfulness, Integrity, Diligence, Competence and Fairness, they provide a compass for the day-to-day work of all staff and employees.

Accreditations:

Quality and Accreditation

Outstanding Quality

  • In 1999, Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein (HIAE) was the first hospital in Latin America to be accredited by the Joint Commission International (JCI), the most important international healthcare accreditation group. This accreditation is a sign of quality care and strong management.
  • In 2003, HIAE was reaccredited by the JCI and in 2006 it became the first Latin American hospital to receive a third consecutive accreditation.
  • In 2007, HIAE’s Stroke Center was the first in Latin America to receive JCI’s certification for specific diseases and conditions.

Other Certifications

  • ISO 14001 - Environment: Morumbi, Alphaville and Jardins.
  • ISO 9001:2000 – Main Hospital, Diagnostic & Preventive Medicine and Volunteer Department.
  • Certification by the American Association of Blood Banks (AABB).

Recognition

  • 94% of our patients are satisfied or very satisfied with HIAE’s services and 95% recommend the hospital.
  • 99% of our doctors claim to be very satisfied or satisfied with HIAE and recommend our services to other doctors. In addition, 98% of them would recommend the hospital to their patients.
  • Intensive care unit was voted best in São Paulo by 41% of the participating doctors.
  • The best hospital in São Paulo according to 43% of participating doctors.

Awards and recognition in 2007

  • Best in class: a survey of 1,000 physicians in diverse specialties conducted by Datafolha (Folha de S. Paulo) ranked HIAE first among 343 hospitals in the city of São Paulo by a wide margin. HIAE also ranked first specifically in ICU, Surgery Center, Emergency Care, and Pediatrics.
  • Transplant record: according to the São Paulo State Department of Health, Einstein set a record for liver transplants, performing 82 in 2007, and was one of the leading transplant centers in São Paulo State.
  • “Valor 1000”: top award from Valor Econômico in the medical services sector, received by HIAE for the sixth year.
  • Award for automation in healthcare from GS1, the leading supply chain standards organization in the world.
  • “Top Hospitalar”: top award in Philanthropic Care from IT Mídia and Fornecedores Hospitalares.
  • DCI survey on social responsibility: ranked first in Health by DCI survey of 100 NGOs
  • BMP Paribas Citizenship award for the Volunteers´ Department.
  • Aberje Brasil 2007: top award in Communication & Press Relations from Brazilian Business Journalism Association (ABERJE).
