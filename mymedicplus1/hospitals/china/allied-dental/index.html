A Glimpse of Kunming

Kunming is the capital city of Yunnan province in China, it is the commercial, cultural and transportation center of Yunnan. Yunnan is situated in southwest China, bordered by Vietnam, Laos, and Burma; it is China's gateway to Southeast Asia. The greater Kunming municipality has over 5 million residents living five municipal regions and 8 counties, with total administrative area of 21,000 square kilometers.


With over 2400 years of history, exotic indigenous cultural heritage, a pleasant climate and beautiful scenery, Kunming is renowned tourist destination for millions of travelers every year, it is also the gateway or staging area to numerous excursions and adventures to the outer province of Yunnan.
People


People of Kunming are known for their warmth and friendliness. Though the city has been going through dramatic economic advancements and modernization, its people and places still retain the same old-fashion laid-back attitude and provincial city atmosphere.


There are over 26 native ethnic groups in Yunnan, each with its own unit and colorful heritage and tradition. Major ethnic groups in Yunnan are Han, Dai, Bai, Naxi, Lisu, Hani, Tibetan, Hui, Jingpo etc. Dai and Bai believe in Hinayana Buddhism, Tibetan and Naxi believe in Lamais Buddhism, Hui people are Muslims and many other ethnic groups believe in their own kind of primal worship.


Climate

Yunnan province is blessed with a year-round mild climate. The capital city of Kunming also known as China's "Spring City", because of its spring like climate throughout the year. It has rich horticultural habitats, lush vegetation, and beautiful blooming flowers perpetually decorated the cityscape, and average temperature in Kunming is 15 degrees Celsius, or 70 degrees Fahrenheit.


Transportation

Kunming International Airport is one of the five major air flight hubs in China. There are frequent flights to all major cities in China, as well as direct international flights to Hong Kong, Thailand, Singapore, Malaysia, Burma, and Korea. There is also daily train service to Sichuan, Guangxi and the Vietnam border. Most major sightseeing areas in Yunnan are also accessible by direct highway link.


Local Flavors

Being at the crossroad of a rich melting pot of cultural diversity, Yunnan's mainstream cuisine reflects its unique blending of spicy Sichuan flavor along with the influence of Southeast Asia's cooking techniques. Each ethnic minority group has its own distinctive and exotic food. Famous food staples in Yunnan are cross-bridge noodles, and steamed pot chicken. If you arrive at the right season, there is bountiful supply of prized exotic mushrooms and fungi which are delicacies that no one should miss.


There a also a wide selection of local Souvenirs that suit every tourist's need. There are many kinds of locally produced products that can only be found here. Some of these are: ethnic minority handicrafts, Pu-Er Tea (a black tea well-known for it's health benefits), red wood animal carvings, jade and precious stone, wax-dyed fabric, native alpine coffee, butterfly sample, and unique kind of local painting style known as "Zhong-cai-hua", which never fail to grab your attention with its saturated colors.


Sightseeing

In addition to numerous sightseeing spots around the region, Yunnan is known as the golf heaven of China. Golf Digest rates some of its courses among the best in China/East Asia, Many of these courses are located in the outskirts of Kunming. There are also many hot-spring resorts situated on the south side of the city.
