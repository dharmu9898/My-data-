Description:

"The Instituto Valenciano de Infertilidad came into being in 1990, as the first medical institution in Spain wholly specialized in human reproduction.

This clinic is at the forefront of Reproductive Medicine and its philosophy is based on maximum personal attention, providing concrete solutions for unique human beings. Our method starts with research into the couple and involves analysis, diagnosis, development of the treatment and its application, up to evaluation of the results, all combined with continuous monitoring that throws light on other therapeutic options, in case it is necessary to change the treatment or repeat it in another cycle. All this coupled with the most modern technology to carry out each technique with the maximum guarantee of success.

We work to satisfy the dreams of couples for responsible, desired paternity, with minimum risk and maximum comfort. However, it is also important that we offer Medicine with a more human face: to provide sensitive psychological counselling, taking into account the emotions and stresses that infertile couples have to contend with.

With the desire to contribute to the welfare of society, the IVI Team has opened centres in several Spanish cities: IVI Almería, IVI Barcelona, IVI Castellón, IVI Madrid, IVI Murcia, and IVI Sevilla, IVI Valencia and IVI Vigo; and outside Spain, IVI México, IVI Lisboa and IVI Santiago de Chile.

We are delighted to welcome you to IVI.es, a meeting point for patients and health care professionals in Spain and the rest of the world. Through this website you can find out who we are and how we can help you, placing all of science and technology at the service of fertility.

If you are a woman, so that you too can have a child; if you are a doctor, for you to know we would like to collaborate with you."

Drs. Antonio Pellicer and José Remohí
Founders of the Instituto Valenciano de Infertilidad

Highlights

Background and Awards

The IVI Team is one of the most prestigious centres in the world and a reference point in everything concerning human reproduction. Our continuous commitment to research has led us to develop new solutions aimed at those couples who are not naturally able to conceive.

Some of the milestones of our history are as follows:

  • The IVI presents at the 22nd Congress of the European Society of Human Reproduction & Embryology (ESHRE) a study on the probability of gestation in a cycle with oocyte donation in women who smoke and in those who do not (June 2006)
  • The Valencia Professional Association of Doctors awards its Gold Medal to Dr. Pellicer (June 2006)
  • The Valencia City Council awards the City’s Gold Medal to IVI (October 2006)
  • The IVI holds the 1st IVI America Congress in Mexico City (November 2006)
  • First pregnancies after vitrification (December 2006)
  • IVI Murcia presents the first Spanish case of two twin babies born free from a type 2 multiple endocrine neoplasy (MEN2A) (January 2007)
  • Preimplantational Genetic Diagnosis (PGD) has made possible the birth in Vigo, Spain, of a baby free from Norrie’s Syndrome for the first time in the world (March 2007)
  • IVI Madrid is ten years old (March 2007)
  • The clinics of the Valencia Infertility Institute (IVI) obtain the ISO 9001:2000 quality certificate (April 2007)
  • As part of its ovule donation programme, the IVI is starting a study that will allow us to detect whether the donors are carriers of Cystic Fibrosis or the Fragile X Syndrome (May 2007)
  • In collaboration with the Teresa Herrera Hospital of A Coruña, Spain, the IVI is carrying out a study that demonstrates that smoking fragments the DNA of semen (May 2007)
  • The IVI clinic in Madrid obtains the Serono Prize for Innovation, Quality, and Image in Assisted Reproduction (June 2007)
  • The IVI opens the doors of its new centre in Santiago, Chile, which is the first infertility clinic in the country (June 2007)
  • The IVI in Barcelona and the Association of Mucopolisacaridosis Sufferers sign a Collaboration Agreement between both entities (June 2007)
  • The Valencia Infertility Institute has been the first in Spain to achieve the first births from vitrified ovules with the Cryo-top technique, which originated in Japan (July 2007)
