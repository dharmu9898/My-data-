Description:

The Royal Melbourne Hospital is the oldest hospital in Victoria, Australia, built just prior to the gold rush era that led to a boom in Melbourne's population. RMH City Campus first began as The Melbourne Hospital in 1848 and RMH Royal Park Campus as the Immigrants' Aid Society in 1853. (See Archives for more historical information.)

The Royal Melbourne Hospital is a privileged member of Melbourne's world-leading Parkville Precinct, and enjoys strong relationships with many of the city's major universities and research institutes.

There are a number of exciting developments underway, including the co-location of the new Royal Women's Hospital at the RMH City Campus site, the development of integrated cancer services and joint projects with community providers to improve service delivery to the communities we serve.

List of Surgeries/Treatments:

Cardiothoracic Surgery

  • Coronary bypass surgery, including total arterial grafting and off-pump surgery
  • Cardiac valve repair and replacement
  • Reconstruction and replacement of the thoracic aorta, including the arch
  • Electrophysiology surgery for life-threatening arrhythmias
  • Correction of congenital cardiac abnormalities in adults

Ophthalmology

  • Anterior segment surgery including cataract and corneal surgery
  • Glaucoma - medical, surgical and laser surgery Orbital, oculoplastic and lacrimal surgery
  • Medical retinal service - diabetic retinopathy, retinal vascular disease and age related macular degeneration.

  • Prosthodontics
  • Hospital dentistry
