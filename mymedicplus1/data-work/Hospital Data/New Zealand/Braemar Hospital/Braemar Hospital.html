Description:

Both of our hospitals offer a caring and supportive environment. They both also offer the highest standards of clinical care through over 100 credentialed specialists, our highly qualified, skilled and dedicated staff and modern technology.

With over 100 surgeons, physicians and anaesthetists credentialed to work at Braemar Hospitals and 8 Operating Theatres we are one of the largest private hospitals in the country.

Braemar Hospital has Certification under the Health and Disability Sector Standards (Safety) Act for a full three year period.

Braemar Day Hospital was the first hospital in New Zealand to gain certification under the new Day-stay surgery and procedures Standard (NZS 8164:2005), also for a full three year period.

In 1970 Braemar Hospital Ltd resolved to transfer to the Braemar Hospital Charitable trust all of the assets of the company on the condition that the hospital operate as a charitable trust.

Under the guidance of the Braemar Hospital Charitable Trust the hospital continued to grow adding new theatres, wards and services.

In 2001 the trust became known as the Braemar Charitable Trust.To cope with an ever-increasing demand for its services a new day hospital was opened in Knox Street in August 2001.

Braemar Hospital is now one of the largest private hospitals in New Zealand offering a comprehensive range of Specialist services.Braemar Hospital Ltd is 100% owned by the Braemar Charitable Trust.

Highlights

Braemar Hospital was judged the inaugural winner of the Excellence in Workforce Development category at the 2004 Waikato Business Excellence Awards.

Braemar Hospital is a proud sponsor of the Waikato Clinical Skills and Simulation Centre at Waikato DHB, and also of the Clinical Practice Award category of the Waikato DHB ‘Best of Health’ Awards.

Surveys conducted by the New Zealand Institute of Economic Research on behalf of the New Zealand Private Hospitals Association in 2003 and 2004 showed that Braemar Hospital fees are amongst the lowest in the country.
