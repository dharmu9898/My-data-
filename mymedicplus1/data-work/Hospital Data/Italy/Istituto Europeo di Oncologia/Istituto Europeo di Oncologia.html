Description:
The European Institute of Oncology was established to implement an innovative model for health and advanced research in the international oncology field. Its Division and Unit Directors come from eight European countries.

Conceived by Umberto Veronesi and inaugurated in May 1994, the Institute became a research hospital and treatment centre (IRCCS or “Istituto di Ricovero e Cura a Carattere Scientifico”) through the Ministerial Decree issued in January 1996. The European Institute of Oncology adopts the non-profit private-law organisation model and provides services through agreements with Italy’s National Health Service.

Accreditations and Awards:

Accreditation by the Joint Commission International

The European Institute of Oncology has been accredited as a healthcare organisation by the Joint Commission International since December 2002.

This authoritative accreditation of excellence reflects a precise and independent assessment of how a healthcare organisation can provide patients with expertise and high-quality care.
IEO was the first cancer centre outside the United States to earn JCI accreditation.

Best workplaces Italy 2006

The European Institute of Oncology has earned the title of “Best Workplace in Italy 2006”. The survey, conducted by the Great Place To Work Institute, which has been present in the United States for over 20 years and is now in its fourth year in Italy, annually awards the title of “Best Workplace in Italy” to the 35 best workplaces in the country. The European Institute of Oncology is ranked 18st on this prestigious list. This important award follows the ones on year 2005 and 2003.
