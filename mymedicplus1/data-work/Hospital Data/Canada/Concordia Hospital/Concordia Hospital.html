Our History

Concordia Hospital was founded in 1928, just over 75 years ago. The Foundation was incorporated as a registered charity in 1975.Concordia Place, a recently constructed 140 bed Personal Care Home, became operational in 2000.

Concordia Hospital and the Concordia Hospital Foundation are each governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. The Mennonite Benevolent Society is the founding body for Concordia Hospital, and by extension, the Foundation.

Over 1,150 employees and 180 volunteers serve Concordia Hospital and Concordia Place, while the Foundation has two employees (an Executive Assistant and a Chief Operating Officer). Concordia volunteers graciously give of themselves as they help us carry out Concordia's mission - care, services and fundraising activities of benefit to our patients and residents.

Highlights

Our Mission

Concordia (Concordia Hospital, Concordia Place, Concordia Foundation and Concordia Village) provides faith-based health and wellness services to the surrounding urban and rural community with quality care in a client-centred environment.

The Foundation's Mission is to support Concordia by promoting the health of the community served by Concordia; by supporting Concordia standards of health services, research and educational activities; and by fundraising for capital projects designed to enhance the care, services and environment provided at Concordia.

List of Surgeries/Treatments:

Surgery Program

  • Concordia is a major centre for hip and knee replacements completing over 1, 300 hip and knee joint replacements.
  • The Surgery Program provides a variety of surgical expertise in the fields of Urology, Orthopedics, Plastics, Oral, Gynecological and General Surgery. We perform elective surgery Monday to Friday and emergency surgery as required.

Physiotherapy

  • Physiotherapists work with inpatients and outpatients to help them achieve their greatest independent level of movement and function. Your Doctor will order physiotherapy if it is necessary.
  • Physiotherapists assess, treat and provide education for a variety of medical and surgical problems. They also recommend walking equipment that is necessary, such as crutches, walkers, and canes.

Respiratory Services

  • Respiratory Therapists provide a range of clinical and technological services to care for patients with cardio-respiratory problems. Therapists work in a variety of settings including intensive care, emergency, chronic and acute care areas, patient education (e.g. Asthma Centre, Home Oxygen Program) and research.
  • Common tests ordered by the Physician may include pulmonary functions (breathing tests), and arterial blood gases (blood drawn and analyzed for oxygen, carbon dioxide levels, and pH status).

Speech Language Pathology

  • Our communication system, which involves hearing, understanding, thinking and expressing plays a vital role in all aspects of everyday life - in our jobs, our families, and our recreation. When communication processes are damaged by disorders of speech, language, or hearing, the effects are always serious. We have a Speech-Language Pathologist on staff to deal with the prevention, identification, diagnosis and rehabilitation of adults with speech, language, cognitive communication, and swallowing impairments.
  • Our Speech-Language Pathologist also takes part in programs and on teams that provide consultative and direct treatment services, counseling of clients and families, and referral to other professionals.

Spiritual Care

  • We are a Mennonite Christian hospital committed to caring for the whole person – spirit, mind and body. A hospital stay often brings out our need for spiritual care. We seek to meet your religious and spiritual needs respecting your faith background or beliefs.
